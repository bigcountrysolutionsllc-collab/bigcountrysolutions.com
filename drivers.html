Driver FAQ

Questions
Answered.

How do I actually get paid? +
Earnings accumulate in your BCS Fuel driver account after each confirmed delivery. You can request a payout at any time and funds are transferred to your bank account. Most drivers receive payment within 1–2 business days.
Do I need special insurance? +
You need a standard personal auto insurance policy. BCS Fuel carries commercial liability coverage that supplements your policy while you're actively on a delivery. We'll walk you through everything during onboarding.
Is fuel handling dangerous? +
When done correctly, fuel delivery is a straightforward process. We provide a full online safety certification before your first delivery. The app guides you through every step. Thousands of fuel deliveries are completed safely every day.
Can I drive in multiple states? +
Yes. Once approved, you can accept orders across all three service states — CT, NY, and NJ. The app will show available orders near your current location automatically.
What kind of vehicle do I need? +
Any reliable truck, SUV, or large van that can safely carry approved portable fuel containers. Your vehicle will go through a basic inspection during onboarding to confirm it meets our safety standards.
How many orders will I actually get? +
Order volume depends on your location, time of day, and how long you stay online. CT, NY, and NJ are three of the highest-demand fuel markets in the country. Drivers in active zones report consistent order flow, especially during mornings and evenings.
Is there a minimum hours requirement? +
None. Work one hour a week or forty. BCS Fuel has zero hour minimums, no shift requirements, and no penalties for inactivity. Your account stays active as long as you want it.
When can I start? +
Most applicants are fully onboarded and taking orders within 48 hours of applying. The process includes a background check, vehicle review, and a 30-minute online safety certification — all done from your phone or computer.
